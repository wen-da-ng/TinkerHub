Heart Rate (Pulse)

Normal: 60–100 bpm at rest
Too High (Tachycardia, >100 bpm): May indicate arrhythmia, heart failure, or stress
Too Low (Bradycardia, <60 bpm): Could be a sign of heart block, aging, or an issue with the heart's electrical system
Blood Pressure (BP)

Normal: Around 120/80 mmHg
High BP (Hypertension, >130/80 mmHg): Increases risk of heart disease, stroke, and artery damage
Low BP (<90/60 mmHg): May indicate heart failure or shock, especially if accompanied by dizziness
Blood Oxygen Level (SpO₂)

Normal: 95–100%
Low SpO₂ (<90%): Can suggest poor heart function, respiratory issues, or circulatory problems
If you notice persistent abnormalities in these vital signs, especially with symptoms like chest pain, shortness of breath, dizziness, or fatigue, it's best to seek medical attention.
